Instructions

You will hear samples of computer generated speech created using different methods. The purpose of this test is to evaluate the quality of each sample, so that we (the researchers) can compare the methods and know which ones sound better to a general audience.

 

Each sample should be given a score according to the following scale, known as the MOS (mean opinion score) scale for naturalness:

Score Quality of the Speech Naturalness
5 Excellent Completely natural
4 Good Mostly natural
3 Fair Equally natural and unnatural
2 Poor Mostly unnatural
1 Bad Completely unnatural

 

Each sample should also be given a pronunciation accuracy score, using the scale below.

Score Accuracy of the Speech Definition
5 Excellent No mispronunciations
4 Good Few minor mispronunciations
3 Fair Many minor mispronunciations
2 Poor Few major mispronunciations
1 Bad Many major mispronunciations
 

 

We have included a reference audio sample here which should rate highly on both scales (it may not be a perfect 5 on both). 

You will also see the transcribed text for the audio sample in the link for each sample (see below), which will be useful for evaluating pronuncations accuracy.  You may find that some audio samples are missing words or even large parts of a sentence; these count as major mispronunciations.

There are a total of 40 HITs, and we prefer you do as many as you can. 

Approval/Rejection Policy

To ensure that you know French well enough that you can evaluate the speech, we require that you also transcribe the reference audio (write down what the speaker is saying in the reference clip) in the text box below. Do not worry about spelling or punctuation. This step is only for validation and is not the main purpose of the study.  It is required that the correct transcription appear for every HIT in the box below, so it may be helpful to transcibe the reference once and keep it in a nearby notepad.  We prefer that the transcription is translated to English, but it may be left in French.

We (the researchers) have statistical methods to detect if you are randomly selecting scores and we will reject HITs that come from these individuals.

To obtain accurate results, we strongly recommend that you wear headphones and work in a quiet environment, otherwise you might not be able to discriminate between files with clearly different features.
